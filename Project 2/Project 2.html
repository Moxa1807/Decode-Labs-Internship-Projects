Business Insights¶

  1. The dataset consists of 1,200 transaction records and is free from missing values and duplicate records, making it suitable for reliable analysis.

  2. Customer purchases are generally made in small quantities, while bulk purchases occur only occasionally.

  3. Printer is the most frequently ordered product, whereas Chair contributes the highest total revenue, demonstrating that higher-priced products can generate greater revenue despite fewer orders.

  4. Online payment is the most preferred payment method, highlighting the growing adoption of digital payment systems.

  5. Order statuses are distributed across Delivered, Pending, Cancelled, Returned, and Shipped, indicating a complete order processing lifecycle. However, reducing cancelled and returned orders could improve overall business performance.

  6. Monthly sales fluctuate throughout the year, suggesting seasonal demand patterns that businesses can use for forecasting and promotional planning.

  7. Correlation analysis confirms that Total Price is primarily influenced by Unit Price and Quantity, emphasizing these two factors as the key drivers of revenue.

  8. Overall, the analysis provides valuable insights into customer purchasing behaviour, product performance, payment preferences, and sales trends, supporting data-driven business decision-making.

Conclusion¶

This project performed Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on an e-commerce sales dataset using Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn. The analysis included data exploration, descriptive statistics, visualization, and correlation analysis to identify meaningful business patterns. The findings highlighted customer purchasing behaviour, product performance, payment preferences, order status distribution, and monthly sales trends. Overall, the project demonstrates how Python can be effectively used to transform raw transactional data into actionable business insights, supporting informed decision-making and strategic planning.
